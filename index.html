What is the ruling on praying behind someone who reads Quran with mistakes?
بسم الله الرحمن الرحيم This is an important question in Islamic jurisprudence. The scholars differ based on the nature of the mistake:

Minor errors (lahn khafi): Prayer is valid, though the imam should improve their recitation.

Major errors (lahn jali) that change meaning — e.g., changing a letter in Al-Fatihah: Most Hanafi, Shafi'i and Hanbali scholars hold the prayer may be invalidated. However, if one cannot find a better imam, the salah remains valid.

Always consult a local scholar for guidance specific to your situation.
JazakAllah khair. Can you quote the relevant hadith?
إِنَّمَا الْأَعْمَالُ بِالنِّيَّاتِ The Prophet ﷺ said: "Actions are judged by intentions" — this applies here too. The best recitation is that done sincerely for Allah. Ibn Mas'ud reported that the Prophet ﷺ said: "Read the Quran, for Allah does not punish a heart which carries the Quran." (Ad-Darimi)
